

# Luiz Bravo

**Interested in the spaces where Statistics, Artificial Intelligence, and Information Retrieval meet.**

I am a Master's student in Computer Science at São Paulo State University (UNESP) and hold a Bachelor's degree in Statistics and Data Science from the University of São Paulo (USP).

My research explores how intelligent systems learn **similarity, context, uncertainty, and relationships**. I am particularly interested in the intersection of Information Retrieval, Deep Metric Learning, Graph Neural Networks, and Bayesian approaches to Machine Learning.

Rather than viewing Artificial Intelligence as a single discipline, I see it as a meeting point between statistics, optimization, networks, information retrieval, and human reasoning. Many of the problems that interest me emerge precisely at these intersections.

---

## What I'm Trying to Understand

My work is driven by a few recurring questions:

* Can uncertainty improve representation learning?
* How can context be incorporated into similarity learning?
* Can probabilistic reasoning improve ranking and retrieval systems?
* How can graph structures enrich visual and semantic representations?
* What can Information Retrieval learn from Statistics and Network Science?

---

## Current Research

### Probabilistic Deep Metric Learning

Developing loss functions that combine contextual similarity, uncertainty estimation, and probabilistic reasoning for image retrieval and representation learning.

### Graph Neural Networks

Studying graph-based approaches for scientific image classification and complex relational data.

### Information Retrieval

Investigating ranking optimization, contextual similarity, pseudo relevance feedback, and retrieval effectiveness.

### Network Science

Exploring graph sampling, structural preservation, and the relationship between network properties and machine learning performance.
